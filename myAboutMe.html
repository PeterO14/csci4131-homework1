What I found most interesting about this chapter is Ebbinghaus’s findings that offered support for the frequency principle. Ebbinghaus found that continuing to study a list beyond the point of mastery, in his case 10 to 20 further repetitions, the savings after 24 hours showed a substantial increase. This practice, of continuing to practice after a perfect performance, has been dubbed Overlearning. Ebbinghaus goes further to demonstrate that Thomas Brown’s principle of frequency, stating that the frequency of pairings affects the strength of an association, is applicable to periods of overlearning as well as periods that show improvement during practice.
